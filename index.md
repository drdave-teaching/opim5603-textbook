This book is the readable companion to **OPIM 5603: Statistics in Business Analytics**, a
fully online, R-based statistics course. Each chapter follows a module of the course;
each section is a lecture, paired with its Google Colab (R) notebook and data files. The
narrative is built from the lecture video transcripts, lightly edited for reading.

> **Taught entirely in R using Google Colaboratory.** Every notebook referenced here is an
> R notebook you can open in Colab.

## About the course
- **Instructor:** Dave Wanik (dave.wanik@uconn.edu)
- **Format:** Fully online, asynchronous · 3 credits · no prerequisites
- **Description:** Advanced exploration of statistical techniques for data analysis —
  population/sample concepts, inference, and estimation — taught entirely in R.

### Learning objectives
By the end of the course you should be able to:
1. Visualize, tabulate and analyze data using R
2. Describe and compute probabilities of probability distributions
3. Conduct hypothesis tests about population means and proportions
4. Perform regression analysis for numeric and binary target variables

## How the book is organized
| Ch. | Module | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Descriptive Statistics | Getting started in R; describing & visualizing data |
| 2 | Probability Distributions | Discrete & continuous distributions; sampling, CLT, CIs |
| 3 | Hypothesis Testing | One- and two-sample inference for means & proportions |
| 4 | Maximum Likelihood Estimation | MLE theory and its link to regression |
| 5 | Regression | Correlation, linear & generalized linear models, chi-square |
