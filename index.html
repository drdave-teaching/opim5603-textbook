Statistics in Business Analytics#

An R-based course companion — OPIM 5603#

by Dave Wanik · University of Connecticut

⚠️ Work in progress

These materials are a living draft — actively being written, revised, and expanded from the lecture videos. Many lecture narratives are still being filled in from transcripts (you’ll see “coming soon” notes). This is a teaching companion, not a final or official reference. It’s a work in progress!

This book is the readable companion to OPIM 5603: Statistics in Business Analytics, a fully online, R-based statistics course. Each chapter follows a module of the course; each section is a lecture, paired with its Google Colab (R) notebook and data files. The narrative is built from the lecture video transcripts, lightly edited for reading.

Taught entirely in R using Google Colaboratory. Every notebook referenced here is an R notebook you can open in Colab.

About the course#

  • Instructor: Dave Wanik (dave.wanik@uconn.edu)

  • Format: Fully online, asynchronous · 3 credits · no prerequisites

  • Description: Advanced exploration of statistical techniques for data analysis — population/sample concepts, inference, and estimation — taught entirely in R.

Learning objectives#

By the end of the course you should be able to:

  1. Visualize, tabulate and analyze data using R

  2. Describe and compute probabilities of probability distributions

  3. Conduct hypothesis tests about population means and proportions

  4. Perform regression analysis for numeric and binary target variables
